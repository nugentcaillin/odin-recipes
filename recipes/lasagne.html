Lasagne

Description

Lasagne is a classic Italian dish made with layers of flat pasta sheets, rich meat or vegetable sauce, creamy béchamel, and melted cheese. It’s baked until golden and bubbly, creating a comforting, hearty meal. The dish is versatile, with variations like vegetarian lasagne or seafood versions.

Originating from Italy, lasagne has become a beloved comfort food worldwide. Its layers meld together during baking, offering a perfect balance of textures and flavors. Whether served at family gatherings or enjoyed as leftovers, lasagne is a timeless favorite.

Ingredients

  • Ground beef or a mix of beef and pork
  • Onion, finely chopped
  • Garlic, minced
  • Canned tomatoes (crushed or diced)
  • Tomato paste
  • Olive oil
  • Dried oregano and basil
  • Salt and pepper
  • Red wine (optional)
  • Sugar (optional, to balance acidity)
  • Butter
  • All-purpose flour
  • Milk
  • Nutmeg (optional)
  • Lasagne sheets (fresh or dried)
  • Mozzarella cheese, shredded
  • Parmesan cheese, grated

Steps

  1. Heat olive oil in a large pan over medium heat. Add chopped onion and minced garlic, sauté until softened.
  2. Add the ground beef (or beef/pork mix) and cook until browned, breaking it up with a spoon.
  3. Stir in tomato paste, canned tomatoes, dried oregano, basil, salt, and pepper. Add a splash of red wine if using.
  4. Simmer the sauce on low heat for 20-30 minutes, stirring occasionally. Taste and adjust seasoning, adding a pinch of sugar if needed to balance acidity.
  5. Melt butter in a saucepan over medium heat. Add flour and whisk continuously for 1-2 minutes to form a roux.
  6. Gradually pour in milk, whisking constantly to avoid lumps. Cook until the sauce thickens.
  7. Season with salt, pepper, and a pinch of nutmeg. Remove from heat and set aside.
  8. Preheat the oven to 375°F (190°C).
  9. In a baking dish, spread a thin layer of meat sauce to prevent sticking.
  10. Add a layer of lasagne sheets, followed by meat sauce, béchamel, and a sprinkle of mozzarella and Parmesan cheese.
  11. Repeat the layers until the dish is full, finishing with a top layer of béchamel and a generous amount of cheese.
  12. Cover the dish with foil and bake for 25 minutes.
  13. Remove the foil and bake for another 15-20 minutes, or until the top is golden and bubbly.
  14. Let the lasagne rest for 10-15 minutes before serving. Garnish with fresh basil or parsley if desired.
